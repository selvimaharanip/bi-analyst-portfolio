## Overview

### Objective

Support strategic portfolio decision-making by evaluating credit utilization efficiency, identifying structural underutilization patterns, and defining activation strategies to enhance capital productivity and sustainable revenue growth.

---

## Context

Using portfolio data from **5,000 active customers**, this analysis shifts focus from traditional portfolio metrics toward **credit utilization efficiency, capital productivity, and monetization depth**.

Although aggregate indicators (**86.38M total spend** and **127.86 average transactions**) appear strong, average utilization remains at only **22.87%**, signaling substantial unused credit capacity across the portfolio.

Rather than focusing solely on transaction volume, this analysis evaluates whether portfolio growth is constrained by credit availability or by customer spending behavior.

Power BI dashboards built from structured analytical datasets are used to assess utilization behavior, segment imbalance, capital productivity, and potential revenue concentration risk.

---

## Key Insights

- Average credit utilization is **22.87%**, indicating underutilized portfolio capacity.
- The majority of customers fall into the **Low Usage** segment, creating a structural monetization imbalance.
- Spending does not scale proportionally with assigned credit limits, suggesting that limit expansion alone is unlikely to generate sustainable growth.
- Long-tenured customers continue to exhibit low utilization levels, demonstrating that customer retention does not necessarily translate into monetization depth.
- The primary growth constraint appears to be **customer utilization behavior rather than customer acquisition, retention, or credit availability**.

---

## Decision Strategy

### Behavioral Activation

- Utilization-based incentives (threshold-triggered rewards)
- Targeted migration from Low → Moderate Usage
- Personalized spending campaigns for underutilized customers

### Portfolio Optimization

- Identify dormant high-limit customers
- Rebalance exposure based on utilization performance
- Improve capital allocation efficiency across customer segments

### KPI Realignment

Shift portfolio monitoring from:

**Traditional Focus**

- Total Spend
- Customer Growth
- Credit Limit Expansion

↓

**Strategic Focus**

- Utilization Efficiency
- Capital Productivity
- Customer Activation
- Segment Migration

## Key Takeaway

This analysis demonstrates how credit portfolio data can be transformed into a structured monetization strategy.

Rather than focusing solely on revenue volume or credit expansion, the framework highlights the importance of:

- **Utilization Efficiency**
- **Behavioral Activation**
- **Capital Productivity**
- **Portfolio Balance**

The findings suggest that future portfolio growth should prioritize activating existing customers and improving utilization depth, enabling data-driven, risk-aware, and sustainable revenue optimization aligned with strategic portfolio management.
